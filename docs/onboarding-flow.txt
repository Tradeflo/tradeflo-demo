Contractor Onboarding Flow — Copy & UX Reference
This document outlines the full onboarding experience for a new contractor signing up to Tradeflo AI. It covers every screen, every message, and every tooltip — written as final copy ready to hand to the developer.
Onboarding Philosophy
The goal of onboarding is one thing: get the contractor to their first accurate quote as fast as possible. Every step exists to serve that goal. We don't ask for information we don't need. We explain why we're asking for what we do need. And we make it easy to skip steps with a clear warning about what they'll lose if they do.
The core insight
A contractor who generates a bad first quote because they skipped setup will blame the product, not themselves. Onboarding protects both the contractor and the product reputation. Getting them set up properly is not a nice-to-have — it is the product working correctly.

Step 3 — Work Log Upload
The most important setup step. This is where the AI learns the contractor's real rates. This screen needs to clearly explain the value without being technical.


3
Upload your work history
The most important step — this calibrates your pricing


Screen title
Calibrate your pricing


Screen sub
Upload a few past invoices or quotes and we'll use them to calibrate the AI to your actual rates — not industry averages. This is the single biggest thing you can do to get accurate quotes.
Why this matters — shown as an info panel on screen
Without work history:
The AI uses general Atlantic Canada market rates. Quotes may vary by $1,000–$2,000 depending on how your real rates compare to the market.
With work history:
The AI learns your actual labour rates and material costs. Quotes are calibrated to how you really price jobs — typically accurate within 5–10%.


Upload label
Drop your past invoices or quotes here


Upload sub
PDF, CSV, Excel, or plain text. Even 2–3 past jobs makes a significant difference. Max 10MB per file.


Accepted formats note
We accept invoices, quotes, job summaries, or any document that shows what you charged and for what.

Post-Signup Email Sequence
Three short emails sent after signup. Written to feel like they're from a real person, not a system.
Email 1 — Sent immediately after signup


Subject
Welcome to Tradeflo AI — one thing to do first


Body
Hey [First name],  Welcome aboard.  One thing before you start quoting — upload a couple of past invoices to your account. It takes two minutes and it's the single biggest thing you can do to make your quotes accurate.  Without them the AI uses general market rates, which might be close or might be off depending on how you price jobs. With them it learns your real numbers.  You can do it here: [Upload work history →]  Zane Tradeflo AI
Email 2 — Sent 48 hours after signup if no work logs uploaded


Subject
Your quotes are using general rates — here's how to fix that


Body
Hey [First name],  Just noticed you haven't uploaded any past invoices yet.  No pressure — but I want to make sure your quotes are actually useful to you. Right now they're based on general market rates for your trade and region. Once you upload even two or three past jobs the AI calibrates to your real numbers and the difference is significant.  Takes about two minutes: [Upload work history →]  Zane Tradeflo AI
Email 3 — Sent after first quote is generated


Subject
How was that quote?


Body
Hey [First name],  You just generated your first quote — how did the numbers look?  If they were off, it's almost always one of two things: the job description needed more detail, or work history hasn't been uploaded yet. Both are easy to fix.  If the numbers looked right, great — that's the product working as intended.  Reply to this email if you have any questions. I read every one.  Zane Tradeflo AI
